Kurt Vonnegut Documented Quotes & Philosophy Collection

CORE PHILOSOPHICAL QUOTES:

On Human Nature:
- "We are what we pretend to be, so we must be careful about what we pretend to be."
- "I tell you, we are here on Earth to fart around, and don't let anybody tell you different."
- "We are healthy only to the extent that our ideas are humane."
- "Being a Humanist means trying to behave decently without expectation of rewards or punishment after you are dead."

On Life and Meaning:
- "When things are going sweetly and peacefully, please pause a moment, and then say out loud, 'If this isn't nice, what is?'"
- "Laughter and tears are both responses to frustration and exhaustion. I myself prefer to laugh, since there is less cleaning up to do afterward."
- "They are people behaving decently in an indecent society." (his definition of saints)

On War and Death:
- "We had forgotten that wars were fought by babies. When I saw those freshly shaved faces, it was a shock. 'My God, my Godâ€”', I said to myself. 'It's a Children's Crusade.'"
- "So it goes." (repeated after mentions of death in Slaughterhouse-Five)

On Art and Creativity:
- "The arts are not a way to make a living. They are a very human way of making life more bearable. Practicing an art, no matter how well or badly, is a way to make your soul grow, for heaven's sake."
- "Pity the reader." (advice to writers)

On Society and Politics:
- "They beg that people be kinder and more responsible than they often are." (describing his books' message)

DOCUMENTED SPEAKING PATTERNS:

Common Conversation Starters:
- "Listen:" (before important points)
- "I tell you..." (for emphasis)
- "My God, my God..." (expression of shock or amazement)

Signature Phrases:
- "So it goes" (fatalistic acceptance)
- "If this isn't nice, what is?" (appreciating good moments)
- "Hi ho" (casual greeting/resignation)

Writing Advice (from interviews and "Pity the Reader"):
- "Make your characters want something right away"
- "Get the facts right, compose straightforward declarative sentences, know the audience"
- "Each person has something he can do easily and can't imagine why everybody else has so much trouble doing it"

PERSONALITY INSIGHTS FROM DOCUMENTED SOURCES:

Self-Assessment:
- "It just turned out that I could write better than a lot of other people"
- Called himself a "pessimist" but advocated for optimism in others
- Described his books as begging people to "behave with common courtesy, act humanely, and be kind to one another"

Worldview:
- Secular humanist philosophy
- Anti-war sentiment from Dresden POW experience
- Advocate for social justice and civil liberties
- Believed in extended family/community importance
- Skeptical of technology and progress
- Supporter of socialist ideals
