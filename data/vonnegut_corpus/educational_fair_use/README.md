# Kurt Vonnegut Corpus Collection

**Compiled:** November 12, 2025  
**Purpose:** Educational use and text analysis

This collection contains transcripts, public domain works, interviews, speeches, and other materials by Kurt Vonnegut gathered from various legitimate online sources.

---

## Contents Overview

**Total Word Count:** Approximately 61,000 words

### Public Domain Works (4 stories)

These short stories are in the public domain in the United States because their copyrights were not renewed.

1. **2 B R 0 2 B** (1962)
   - Source: Project Gutenberg
   - A satirical science fiction story about population control and voluntary death
   - ~5,661 words

2. **The Big Trip Up Yonder** (1954)
   - Source: Project Gutenberg
   - Also known as "Tomorrow and Tomorrow and Tomorrow"
   - Science fiction about anti-aging technology and family dynamics
   - ~6,721 words

3. **Unready to Wear** (1954)
   - Source: Wikisource
   - Science fiction about people learning to exist without their bodies
   - ~5,791 words

4. **Harrison Bergeron** (1961)
   - Source: Public domain text archive
   - Dystopian short story about enforced equality
   - ~2,221 words

---

### Interviews (3 major interviews)

1. **Progressive Magazine Interview**
   - Interviewer: David Barsamian
   - Topics: Politics, George W. Bush administration, war, history
   - ~2,734 words

2. **Playboy Interview (1973)**
   - Comprehensive interview covering writing, politics, and philosophy
   - Topics: Writers as evolutionary cells, Darwinism, Vietnam War, creative process
   - ~16,608 words

3. **PBS NOW with David Brancaccio (2005)**
   - Topics: Iraq War, education, happiness, Uncle Alex stories, environment
   - Includes Vonnegut's environmental poem
   - ~4,211 words

---

### Commencement Speeches and Lectures (5 speeches)

1. **Bennington College (1970)**
   - Pessimistic yet humorous address about civilization and superstition
   - Famous quote: "To weep is to make less the depth of grief"
   - ~2,756 words

2. **Hobart and William Smith Colleges (1974)**
   - On the need for new religion and dealing with knowledge
   - Discussion of Eden as metaphor for modern world
   - ~4,934 words

3. **Syracuse University (1994)**
   - "Generation A" speech
   - Themes: Extended families, gratitude, love
   - "If this isn't nice, what is?"
   - ~1,465 words

4. **Agnes Scott College (1999)**
   - On the Code of Hammurabi vs. Christian forgiveness
   - Humanism and teaching as noble profession
   - ~998 words

5. **Shape of Stories Lecture**
   - Famous lecture on narrative structure
   - Analysis of story arcs including Cinderella, Hamlet, Kafka
   - ~1,959 words
